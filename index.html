About this place

Our place sits on 20 acres in the Davis Mountains of West Texas, at about 5,000 feet elevation. Fort Davis is one of the least light-polluted areas in the lower 48 — the night sky here is something you have to see to believe. We've watched meteor showers from the back porch and caught star parties at McDonald Observatory, which is 10 minutes up the road.

The house sleeps up to 6: a primary bedroom, a second room with a pull-out sofa, and two twin cots that can go anywhere. The Skoolie — a converted school bus — adds a queen bed sleeping option when it's available (more on that below). With camping gear on the 20 acres, the whole place can host up to 12.

Fort Davis itself is a town of about 1,200 people. There's more to do in the surrounding area than you might expect: Big Bend is 2+ hours south, Marfa is 40 minutes away, Balmorhea's famous spring-fed pool is 37 minutes north. And The Rendezvous — bar, made-to-order pizza, pickleball, live music — is right in town.

This is a place to decompress. Come ready to slow down, look up, and explore.

What's nearby

🔭
10 min away
McDonald Observatory
UT affiliate with regular star parties and solar viewing. One of the best stargazing sites in the country. Book tickets in advance — they fill up.
🥾
5 min away
Davis Mountains State Park
2,700 acres of trails, bird watching, and the famous Davis Mountains Scenic Loop drive. The view from the top does not disappoint.
🏛️
15 min away
Fort Davis National Historic Site
One of the best-preserved frontier military posts in the West. Walking tours, museum, and a lot of "wait, this is actually cool" moments.
🌊
37 min away
Balmorhea State Park
The world's largest spring-fed swimming pool. Crystal clear, snorkeling-worthy, and genuinely refreshing in the middle of the desert. While you're there: Matta's Burger Place has curly fries that are not to be missed.
🎨
~40 min away
Marfa
The arts scene. The Prada Marfa. The Chinati Foundation. The Marfa Lights. It's a vibe. Go at least once while you're out there.
🏔️
~2+ hours away
Big Bend National Park
Chisos Mountains, hot springs on the Rio Grande, and a day trip across the border to the Mexican village of Boquillas (bring your passport). Worth the drive.
🍕
15 min away
The Rendezvous
Bar, made-to-order pizza (actually really good), pickleball, live music. The local hotspot. Go here.
